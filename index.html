    • The Sun

    If we could capture solar energy with 100% efficiency, and area the size of Rhode Island could power the world.

    Right now we can utilize it better to power our appliances, light up our rooms, and capture it's heat to release later.

    The Rain

    Growing cities in desert environemnts are putting increasing strain on our lakes and rivers. This is one resource we cannot do without.

    Captured rainwater can be filtered and used to drink, wash dishes, and shower. Caputred graywater from showers and sinks can be used to flush toilets, water plants, and cool rooms to multiply the gallon per gallon value of our water.

    The Place

    The right choice depends on the right location. Using local building materials supports local economies and reduces the resources required to get them to the build site. Local plants are already adapted to the environment and require less resources to maintain.

    Hot locales benefit from using lighter colors to reflect more heat. Cooler locations can use geothermal heat to stay warm. South facing windows let natural light in.
